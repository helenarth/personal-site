Buffalo Botanical Gardens

Gallery App & Guessing Game

The Buffalo Botanical Gardens is home to more than 10,000 plants, playing a significant role in educating the community of botany. The staff at the gardens recently decided to start documenting all of their plants electronically and has an api to all of their plant information. To help improve the garden experience, I have helped develop a guessing game and plant gallery app to encourage education and engagement at the gardens. The gallery app is being developed as a phonegap app to be deployed to the App Store and Play Store this fall. Click on the images to the right to look at the games

Web Scraper

Radio 2 Drive Web Scraper

Buffalo being as close to Canada as it is, we often pick up a number of Canadian radio stations. One of the best radio shows around is Radio 2 Drive, which is a part of CBC (Canada's version of NPR). Their setlist is... well... music to the ears (yes, yes I know this is a bit corny). But at any rate, they have an awesome setlist they post here on a daily basis. Yet rather than checking the setlist every day I decided to write a screen scraper in Node.js that would grab all the songs for the day and add them to a playlist on Ex.FM. If you're interested in the code base then check out the github repo. Otherwise, check out the playlist on Exfm!

Internships

Center For Computational Research & Voxer

While attending the University at Buffalo I partook in two internship opportunities. The first was with The Center For Computational Research, where I helped develop an internal tool for geologists. Written in Java/Image Magick, "Puffin" (part of VHub) showed a trajectory for volcanic ashes based on wind trajectory, plume height and various other factors.

I was also fortunate enough to spend the Summer of 2012 in San Francisco at Voxer, the world's LARGEST installation of node. At Voxer I worked with the other interns to develop an internal metrics tool using D3.js. I also worked with the Head of Growth sending out consumer surveys and leading usability testing.
